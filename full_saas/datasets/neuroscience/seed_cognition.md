Working memory depends on distributed prefrontal and parietal networks. Sleep loss commonly reduces working memory capacity and increases lapses of attention. Acute stress can increase arousal but often harms executive control when demands require inhibition or flexible response selection. Simple reaction time is sensitive to alertness, fatigue, and sustained attention. Stroop interference reflects conflict monitoring and inhibitory control, frequently associated with anterior cingulate and prefrontal control systems. These relationships are probabilistic and should be interpreted longitudinally rather than diagnostically.
